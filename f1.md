# DIGRESSION: RECOGNITION AND EXPECTATION

<Keypress-audio src="/sw-orig.mp3" playKey="1" />
<Keypress-audio src="/sw-cover.mp3" playKey="2" />

<div class="space-y-5">

<div v-click>
  <h3>Effect of contextual repetition</h3>
  <div class="text-sm">We develop a preference for the familiar; the more we hear the original in a specific context, the harder it is to accept a new version.</div>
  <a href="https://doi.org/10.18290/rpsych.2019.22.2-4" target="_blank" class="text-xs opacity-40 block">doi.org/10.18290/rpsych.2019.22.2-4</a>
</div>

<div v-click>
  <h3>Memory bias toward original sound profile</h3>
  <div class="text-sm">The brain stores the original as a "blueprint"; when a cover deviates from this mental map, it creates a cognitive conflict.</div>
  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1525/mp.2009.27.1.43" target="_blank" class="text-xs opacity-40 block">doi.org/10.1525/mp.2009.27.1.43</a>
</div>

<div v-click>
  <h3>Nostalgia and emotional activation</h3>
  <div class="text-sm">Original music acts as a "time capsule"; covers often lack the deep personal and emotional connections we've built with our memories.</div>
  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1037/a0019006" target="_blank" class="text-xs opacity-40 block">doi.org/10.1037/a0019006</a>
</div>

<div v-click>
  <h3>Mood congruency effects</h3>
  <div class="text-sm">We anchor music to our internal mood; if a cover changes the emotional "vibe," it no longer matches our stored memory of that feeling.</div>
  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0251692" target="_blank" class="text-xs opacity-40 block">doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0251692</a>
</div>

<div v-click>
  <h3>Emotional incongruence and processing cost</h3>
  <div class="text-sm">It requires more mental energy to process a version that "feels wrong," leading to an automatic decrease in perceived likability.</div>
  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1080/15213269.2020.1713164" target="_blank" class="text-xs opacity-40 block">doi.org/10.1080/15213269.2020.1713164</a>
</div>

</div>
